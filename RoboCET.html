1. The robot should fit inside a box of 50x50x30 cm.
2. The weight of the bot should not exceed 20 kg.
3. The bot can be wired or wireless.All the wires should be stacked as a single unit.

5. Power supply can be on board/off board.

Line Tracer

The competition is conducted as two levels.
In the first level, the bot must complete a line track in minimum time.The track consists of acute angles, obtuse angles, right angles and smooth curves.Top five teams completing the 1st round will be promoted to the next round.
The second round is a 'Trap the rat' track.This round consists of a line following track with black and white stripes blocking the path, representing rats and rat traps respectively.The bot must indicate the detection of the rat and the trap by sounding a buzzer/LED.The objective of the bot is to count the number of rats and rat traps and to indicate the number of free rats (No. of rats - No. Of traps) using a buzzer/LED/other methods. Time taken to complete the task will be considered only if the indicated answer is correct.

1. Only five teams will participate in round 2.
2. The method used to indicate the number of free rats in round 2 will be put under scrutiny by the Competition committee to prevent any malpractice.
3. Please don't use ready-made bot-kits for the competition - you will be disqualified.
4. The bot must fit in a box of 20 cm x 20 cm x 20 cm
5. Inter-college teams are allowed.
6. Decisions of the competition committee is final and binding.
